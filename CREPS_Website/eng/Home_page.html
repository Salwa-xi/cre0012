About the center

Saudi Arabia has one of the largest power grids in the world, with a total installed capacity of 74.3 gigawatts (GW) and an annual growth rate of 6.7% over the five years to 2016. The grid serves more than 8.6 million customers across 5,161 km of transmission networks and 70,300 km of distribution networks. By 2021, the installed capacity is expected to increase to 91 GW and the number of customers to 10.8 million. As demand for energy increases, environmental challenges, rising oil prices and climate change concerns are driving the Kingdom to expand its use of renewable energy sources.

Our vision

Establish a research center of excellence in the field of renewable energy and electrical power systems as a high-quality national, regional and international research community.

Our mission

Develop innovative technologies to ensure sustainable energy provision by supporting research programs in the field of renewable energy and electrical power systems at King Abdulaziz University to find technical and procedural solutions that meet the country's future energy needs.

National Renewable Energy Program

The National Renewable Energy Program is a strategic initiative under the umbrella of Saudi Vision 2030 and the King Salman Renewable Energy Initiative. The program aims to maximize the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia's share of renewable energy production. The program has embarked on a specific and consistent roadmap to diversify local energy sources, stimulate economic development, and work towards sustainable economic stability in the Kingdom in light of the objectives of Saudi Vision 2030, which include establishing a renewable energy industry and supporting the development of this promising sector by working to fulfill the Kingdom's commitments to reduce carbon dioxide emissions.
